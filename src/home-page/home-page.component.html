Welcome

The current project is concerned to provide a definition and method for testing what is identified here as Principle Based Design (PBD). As a part of the research a narrative will be developed to show the historical lineage of PBD with reference to the history of waterwheel design and the concept of “Principle” developed in the General Systems Theories of René Descartes (1596-1650) and Howard T. Odum (1924-2002).
The aim here is to identify how these thinkers defined the concept of a “Principle” and how it was relevant to the transformation of design practice in the early modern and modern periods. In doing so the project will then aim to specify a series of Experiments that might be conducted with the aid of a 3D printer to verify that the concept of Principle Based Design has merid.
Through these experiments the intention will be to see whether we can answer the following questions: What is a Principle? What is PBD? How can we demonstrate PBD? Is there a method for PBD? Inasmuch the research falls under a part of practice based design known as Experimental Design Philosophy.
